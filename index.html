    About the Toledo Vibrancy Initiative

    A City of Toledo program to support revitalization that creates jobs, reduces blight, and preserves and improves structures across the city. In 2026, $630,000 was awarded across 19 grants to 17 properties in 10 neighborhoods — expected to leverage roughly $13.5M in private investment.

    The three 2026 grant programs

    Façade Improvement Grant Façade
    Up to $30,000 per building, per year · reimburses 50% of eligible exterior costs (75% in a Legacy Commercial Corridor)

    Catalyzes visual, street-facing improvements that enhance neighborhood character. Eligible work includes exterior code upgrades (building, fire, accessibility), signage, storefronts, windows, lighting, masonry, and painting. Permits required; costs incurred before the grant agreement is executed are not eligible.

    White Box Grant White Box
    Up to $75,000 per building, per year · reimburses 50% of eligible interior costs (75% in a Legacy Commercial Corridor)

    Prepares vacant ground-floor commercial spaces for occupancy by a new business. Eligible work includes interior code upgrades, demolition, drywall, electrical/plumbing/HVAC rough-in, life-safety compliance, ADA improvements, and restaurant-specific build-out. Exterior work, furniture, equipment, inventory, and property acquisition are not eligible.

    Planning Grant Planning
    Supports architectural and design work to activate ground-floor commercial spaces

    Provides financial support to small businesses and property owners seeking to activate ground-floor commercial spaces for occupancy by a new business — particularly in historically significant legacy commercial corridors.

    Key 2026 deadlines for awardees

    Grant agreement executed byFri, May 22, 2026
    Construction must commence byFri, Aug 14, 2026
    Full project completion byFri, Dec 4, 2026

    Failure to begin work by the construction deadline may result in termination of the grant agreement.

    What recipients agree to

    • Provide W-9, Vendor Registration, commercial building insurance, entity operating agreement, and proof of funds / bank commitment letter before the grant agreement is executed.
    • Schedule a pre-development meeting with the City's Economic Development Department.
    • Execute a Restrictive Covenant with the Lucas County Recorder — the property may not be sold without the City's approval before December 1, 2028.
    • Complete the full scope of work and obtain a Certificate of Zoning Compliance (or Certificate of Completion).
    • Submit itemized invoices, proof of payment, and photographs of completed work to receive reimbursement.
    • Maintain good standing with the City of Toledo Division of Taxation, Public Utilities, Code Compliance, and Lucas County property taxes throughout the project.

    Application status

    2026 applications are closed. The application process is administered through ZoomGrants; the Vibrancy Incentive Committee scores applications and the Mayor makes final awards. Questions: Vibrancy@toledo.oh.gov.
