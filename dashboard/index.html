      19 Vibrancy Initiative grants awarded — $13.5M private investment expected across 10 neighborhoods

      Mayor Wade Kapszukiewicz and Chief Growth Officer Brandon Sehlhorst announced the 2026 Vibrancy Initiative grant awards. From a $630K pool, 19 projects were selected from 82 applications across White Box, Facade Improvement, and Planning Grant tracks. The slate: 11 awards on Legacy Commercial Corridors, 10 facades restored, 10 long-vacant storefronts reactivated, 8 existing businesses supported, 5 new businesses attracted.

      Why it matters: The single largest concentrated burst of small-business reinvestment money the city distributes each year. The 22× leverage ratio ($13.5M private on $630K public) is the metric the city uses to justify the program politically. The specific 19 properties are the leading indicator of where activity will visibly shift in 12–18 months.

      Huron Yards phase two nearing completion

      Warehouse Dist

      Crane Development's Huron Yards is on track to wrap phase two in 2026. The former Alleyway building at 28 N. Erie St. is being renovated into a new restaurant called The Station, and a grocery store is taking the ground floor of the old Okun Produce building. Resident parking and the upcoming beer garden's site work are finished.

      Why it matters: A grocery store in the Warehouse District is the missing-amenity piece that turns downtown-adjacent districts from "lively at night" into "lively all day." Strengthens the case for the next residential phase.

      $23M Vistula Metropark advances — design consultant hired

      Vistula

      Metroparks Toledo is advancing what will become its 20th park: a $23M riverfront park along Water Street between Olive and Magnolia. The Blade reported (April 22) Metroparks hired a design consultant; some elements expected to open later in 2026.

      Why it matters: Public green space is the most consistent leading indicator for private investment in adjacent parcels in Toledo. Expect land-banking activity within a 4–6 block radius of Water Street over the next 12 months.

      Uptown / Junction

      Connecting Toledo Neighborhoods to Opportunity — $53M, 38-block redevelopment in design

      UptownJunction

      The federally-funded $53M project covering 38 blocks (13th–21st Street, Adams to Monroe) is in design through 2026, construction 2027–2029, completion targeted 2031. Funded by a $20M USDOT RAISE grant.

      Why it matters: Biggest neighborhood-scale infrastructure investment in this part of Toledo in a generation. Junction's 45% vacancy rate means infrastructure is going in ahead of private development — a deliberate "build it and they will come" bet.
      City of Toledo — RAISE Toledo City Paper
      Downtown

      Owens Corning commits $250M and 30-year downtown HQ stay

      Downtown

      In exchange for a property tax incentive extension the city is pursuing, Owens Corning will invest $250M in building and technology improvements, create 25 new jobs, maintain 1,036 existing high-wage jobs, and commit to keeping its world HQ downtown for 30 more years.

      Why it matters: The single most consequential variable for downtown property values and lunch-trade businesses. A 30-year commitment removes a tail risk that has hung over downtown for two decades.
      The Blade WTOL

      DowntownJunction
